Whale tracking and rescuing system

WTRS provides logistical and practical expertise and equipment for the rescue of whales. We endeavour to be effective communicators and facilitators, maintaining and building relationships and networks while striving to be the leading and most successful cetacean rescue crew in Europe.

WTRS offers specialised hardware, mobile & desktop applications in order to achieve.

  • Whale safety.
  • Better understanding of whale behavior.
  • Lower rates of whale related accidents.
  • Wider knowledge of this animal species.
  • Insight into whale population sizes and migrations
  • Insight into risky situations and accidents related to whale safety

Overview

With reports of entangled, poisoned whales and other whale related accidents dramatically increasing in recent years, WTRS was founded to organize, unify, and advance the endangered whale response network all over the globe, with a focus on best practices, prevention, and safety. Our organization also works to increase awareness of this issue and thereby lead to increased reporting of endangered, whales in critical condition or unsupervised whales.

All data is collected from the hardware devices for whale tracking. Suction cups hold the device to a whale's back,painpessly, where it records data such as depth, water, temperature, and underwater sounds.

Response is only part of the mission, however. WTRS’s main long-term goal is to work toward prevention of future accidents. We aim to use what we learn on responses, including written, photographic, and video documentation, behavioral analysis, and consultation with experts in the field, to better inform these preventative efforts

LET'S MAKE A #PLASTICPACT!

As plastics break down into small pieces, they are eaten by plankton and fish. Whales eat these fish and accumulate plastics in their own bodies. Evidence is mounting that whales are an essential part of the ecosystem by providing us with oxygen, supporting fish stocks, and combatting climate change and all because of where they take their bathroom breaks. We need whales and plastic is not whale food.

Your end of the bargain: For your 2018 New Year's resolution, reduce your use of plastic in your everyday life to help the entire ocean ecosystem.
